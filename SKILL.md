# Top-Tier Perovskite Paper Writing

Write perovskite solar cell (PSC) research manuscripts in the professional style of Nature Energy, Nature Communications, Science, ACS Energy Letters, and Advanced Materials. Extracted from close reading of 10 recent high-impact PSC papers.

## Core Writing Principles

Follow these 10 principles in every section:

1. **Funnel structure**: Every section moves from broad context to specific findings.
2. **Quantitative precision**: All claims backed by specific numbers with units and conditions.
3. **Balanced confidence**: Hedge in mechanism interpretation; be confident in empirical results.
4. **Systematic comparison**: Every result explicitly compared to control/baseline.
5. **Multi-technique validation**: Results corroborated by multiple independent characterization methods.
6. **Protocol compliance**: Name standardized testing protocols explicitly (ISOS-L-2, ISOS-T-3, IEC).
7. **Causal chain construction**: Explain mechanisms through sequential cause-effect sentences.
8. **Novelty signaling**: Use consistent gap vocabulary ("underexplored," "remains limited," "knowledge gap").
9. **Impact framing**: Connect molecular-level innovation to device-level metrics to commercialization potential.
10. **Forward-looking closure**: Conclusions always end with broader implications or future directions.

## Section-by-Section Structure

### Abstract (6-sentence template)

1. **Context** (1-2 sentences): Broad importance of PSCs
2. **Problem** (1 sentence): Specific unresolved challenge
3. **Contribution** (1-2 sentences): "Here we report/demonstrate..."
4. **Mechanism** (1 sentence): Brief how-it-works explanation
5. **Key results** (2-3 sentences): Quantitative highlights (PCE, retention %)
6. **Broader impact** (1 sentence): Significance for commercialization/deployment

Example opening patterns:
- "Metal halide perovskite solar cells combine high power density with low-cost manufacturing, but durability under repeated extreme temperature cycling remains insufficiently understood."
- "Realizing stable and scalable perovskite solar cells (PSCs) under real-world outdoor conditions remains a challenge for deployment."

### Introduction (5-paragraph template)

1. **Broad opening** (1-2 paragraphs): PSC promise + commercialization context
2. **Specific stressor** (1-2 paragraphs): The particular degradation mechanism or challenge
3. **Prior approaches** (1 paragraph): What others have done + their limitations
4. **Gap statement** (1-2 sentences): What is missing in current knowledge
5. **"Here we..." paragraph** (final paragraph): Full contribution summary

Key Introduction patterns:
- Opening: "Perovskite solar cells (PSCs) have emerged as a promising technology in the photovoltaic (PV) sector, possessing high efficiency and low production costs."
- Gap: "However, [specific challenge] remains [a key challenge / insufficiently understood / underexplored]"
- Contribution: "Here we report a strategy to improve..., achieving simultaneous improvement in A and B"

### Results

- Present quantitative findings with tight syntactic patterns pairing data with interpretation
- Always juxtapose control vs. target in the same sentence
- Use retention framing: "retained X% of initial PCE after Y hours/cycles"
- Include certification parentheticals: "27.2% (certified as 26.9%)"
- Champion vs. average distinction must always be explicit

### Discussion / Mechanism

- Build causal chains: "X induces Y, leading to Z and, ultimately, W"
- Use molecular-level descriptions: "Upon [stimulus], [electrons/bonds] are [excited/weakened], thereby facilitating [process]"
- Attribute observations: "This is attributed to..." / "We attribute X to Y"
- Hedge appropriately: "may originate from," "might stem from," "could critically impact"

### Conclusion (5-element template)

1. "In conclusion" / "In summary" opening
2. Restate the problem (1 sentence)
3. Summarize mechanism (1-2 sentences)
4. Key quantitative results (2-3 sentences)
5. Broader significance / future outlook (1-2 sentences)

Closing patterns:
- "We believe that the dynamic regulation using photoswitchable isomeric compounds marks an advancement towards the practical application of perovskite PVs in real-world scenarios."
- "Looking forward, further improvements in thermal fatigue resistance may be achieved through the rational design of multifunctional molecular additives..."

## Rhetorical Moves

### Gap Statements

Use these to identify what is missing in current knowledge:
- "Despite progress, X remains underexplored"
- "has received limited attention in..."
- "represents a significant knowledge gap"
- "most reported studies focus on X, while Y remain limited"
- "it is difficult to fundamentally [eliminate/address]... from a physical perspective"

### Contribution Statements

Use these to declare what the paper uniquely offers:
- "Here we report / demonstrate / employ..."
- "In this study/work, we [demonstrate/introduce/develop]..."
- "This study presents a [facile] strategy to..."
- "To mitigate [X], we introduce [Y]"
- "Our strategy improves the [operational stability] and [commercial viability] of..."

### Mechanism Descriptions

Build cause-effect chains with precise vocabulary:
- "By doing X, we achieve Y through Z"
- "Upon X, Y occurs, leading to Z, thereby facilitating W"
- "X not only A, but also B, reducing C and improving D"
- Semicolon-joined cause-effect with pivot: "...; yet, constrained by X, enormous residual stress is generated"

### Hedging vs. Confidence

| Context | Tone | Phrases |
|---------|------|---------|
| Mechanism interpretation | Cautious | "may originate from," "might stem from," "presumably," "we believe," "could critically impact" |
| Empirical results | Confident | "We further demonstrate," "Our experiments reveal," "These findings conclusively demonstrate" |
| Prior work gaps | Balanced | "Despite progress," "Although X, Y remains..." |

## Data Reporting Conventions

### Efficiency
- One decimal place: "27.2%" not "27%"
- Certified values in parentheses: "27.2% (certified as 26.9%)"
- Champion vs. average always distinguished
- Reverse/forward scan reported separately or as "27.2 (27.1)%"

### Stability
- Percentage retention format: "retained X% of initial PCE"
- Always specify: time + conditions (temperature, atmosphere, light intensity)
- Protocol naming: "ISOS-L-2 protocol," "ISOS-T-3 procedure"
- Error statistics: "mean +/- SD," "n = 6"

### Comparisons
- Control vs. target juxtaposed in same sentence with "while" / "whereas" / "compared to"
- Numerical deltas always stated: "from 1.17 to 1.20 V"

## Journal-Specific Style Differences

| Feature | Nature Energy | Nature Commun. | ACS Energy Lett. | Adv. Mater. |
|---------|--------------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Voice | Predominantly "we" | Mix "we" + passive | Direct "we" | Mix passive + "we" |
| Sentence length | Long, complex | Moderate | Shorter, punchier | Long, multi-clause |
| Hedging | Moderate | Moderate | Low | High |
| Figure refs | "(Fig. 1a)" | "(Fig. 1a)" | "(Figure 1A)" | "(Figure 1a)" |
| Supplementary | "Supplementary Fig. X" | "Supplementary Fig. X" | "Figure SX" | "Figure SX" |
| Conclusion | "We believe..." forward | "we have developed..." | Brief, results-focused | "we effectively mitigated..." |

## Transition Phrases Quick Reference

| Function | Phrases |
|----------|---------|
| Introducing results | "As shown in Fig. X," / "We observed" / "We found that" |
| Adding evidence | "Furthermore," / "Moreover," / "Additionally," |
| Contrasting | "In contrast," / "However," / "whereas" / "Conversely," |
| Drawing conclusions | "These results indicate that..." / "Taken together, our results suggest..." |
| Attributing cause | "We attribute X to Y" / "This is attributed to..." / "stemming from" |
| Signaling novelty | "To the best of our knowledge, this is the first report of..." |
| Projecting impact | "We believe that..." / "This approach not only X but also Y" |
