# Sentence Pattern Templates for Perovskite PSC Papers

Extracted from Nature Energy, Nature Communications, Science, ACS Energy Letters, and Advanced Materials papers. Use these as structural templates — replace the specific content while preserving the syntactic pattern.

## A. Opening / Framing Sentences

These establish research context. Pattern: broad significance -> specific challenge -> knowledge gap.

### "X remains a challenge" pattern
> "Realizing stable and scalable perovskite solar cells (PSCs) under real-world outdoor conditions remains a challenge for deployment."

### "X have emerged as a promising technology" pattern
> "Perovskite solar cells (PSCs) have emerged as a promising technology in the photovoltaic (PV) sector, possessing high efficiency and low production costs."

### "X combines A with B, but C remains insufficiently understood" pattern
> "Metal halide perovskite solar cells combine high power density with low-cost manufacturing, but durability under repeated extreme temperature cycling remains insufficiently understood."

### "Due to X, Y has reached Z" pattern
> "Due to their exceptional photoelectric properties and ease of fabrication, the certified power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) of perovskite solar cells (PSCs) have reached 26.1%, matching that of commercial silicon solar cells at a lower cost."

### "X are a promising Y, but to Z, A and B must be achieved" pattern
> "Halide perovskite semiconductors are a promising emerging photovoltaic (PV) technology, but to rapidly reach the commercialization stage, performance and upscaling must be achieved along with demonstrated reliability in operation and under conditions exceeding the testing conditions."

### "Amid X, the development of Y has become Z" pattern
> "Amid the continuously growing global energy demand and intensifying environmental concerns, the development of highly efficient, stable, and cost-effective solar cell technologies has become a central research focus for scientists worldwide."

## B. Mechanism Description Sentences

Explain physical/chemical mechanisms using causal language, sequential logic, and precise vocabulary.

### "By doing X, we achieve Y through Z" pattern
> "By integrating photoswitchable isomers at grain boundaries, we suppress lattice bond rupture and defect accumulation during repeated light cycling through light-triggered dynamic damage release."

### Contrast structure "In the dark... In contrast..."
> "In the dark, Ca-Abz predominantly adopts the planar E form, stabilized by both its intrinsic molecular orbital configuration and interfacial bonding with the perovskite GB. In contrast, the Z-form is twisted due to steric repulsion between adjacent rings, disrupting pi-conjugation and increasing the molecular orbital energy."

### "Upon X, Y occurs, leading to Z, thereby facilitating W"
> "Upon UV illumination, n(N) electrons are excited to the pi*(N=N) antibonding orbital, weakening the N=N bond and lowering the rotational barrier, thereby facilitating E -> Z photoisomerization."

### "This X allows Y to Z, effectively mimicking..."
> "This reversible E<->Z transformation allows Ca-Abz to dynamically respond to lattice strain, effectively mimicking the perovskite contraction and expansion during light cycling and acting as a buffer."

### "Their X results in Y, making them Z"
> "Their poor thermal conductivity (in comparison with conventional photovoltaic materials such as silicon) results in heat accumulation and the formation of hotspots in solar cell absorbers, making them extremely prone to thermal degradation."

### "We identify that X results in Y, leading to Z that accelerates W"
> "We identify that their poor thermal conductivity results in insufficient thermal transfer, leading to heat accumulation within the absorber layer that accelerates thermal degradation."

### "X induces Y, leading to Z and, ultimately, W"
> "Repeated temperature cycling induces volumetric expansion and contraction within the solar cell stack, leading to mechanical fatigue and, ultimately, delamination, crack formation, or failure at weakly bonded interfaces."

### "X induces Y, leading to degradation at Z"
> "Mismatched thermal expansion between the perovskite absorber and glass substrate induces biaxial tensile strain, leading to degradation at the substrate-perovskite interface and within grain boundaries."

### "X not only A, but also B, reducing C and improving D"
> "The P-O bonds in KPP not only form coordination bonds with Pb2+, but also form hydrogen bonds with FA+, reducing the defect state density and improving the stability of the device."

### Semicolon-joined cause-effect chain with "yet" pivot
> "This significant CTE mismatch causes the perovskite lattice to undergo considerable shrinkage when the device is cooled from the annealing temperature to room temperature; yet, constrained by the minimal shrinkage of the underlying substrate, enormous residual tensile stress is generated inside the film."

## C. Result Presentation Sentences

Present quantitative findings pairing numerical data with interpretive language.

### "As a result, X retains Y% after Z, and delivers PCE of A%"
> "As a result, the PSCs retain over 95% of their initial performance after 2,000 h of ultraviolet-containing light cycling at 65 degrees C and 500 thermal cycles between -40 degrees C and 85 degrees C, and deliver a power conversion efficiency of 27.2% (certified as 26.9%)."

### Direct comparison "X is A%, compared to B% for Y"
> "The PCE of the champion target device is 27.2%, compared to 25.6% for the champion control device."

### "Under [condition], X retained Y%, whereas Z degraded by W%"
> "Under LED light cycling, the target device retained almost 100% of its initial efficiency after 40 days, whereas the control devices degraded by about 1.5% after the same exposure time."

### "X retained Y% after Z h, while W maintained only V% after U h"
> "The o-CB treated devices retained 80% of the original PCE after 1080 h, while the pristine devices maintained only 40% of the initial PCE after ~200 h."

### Compact parenthetical format for dual-scan results
> "We achieved a champion PCE of 27.2 (27.1)% for the tandem solar cells, with VOC of 2.16 (2.15), JSC of 15.6 (15.7) mA/cm2 and FF of 81 (80)% under reverse (forward) scan direction."

### Parallel structure with "respectively" and "while" contrast
> "The n-i-p and p-i-n PSCs based on OAI-1 still maintain 72% and 81% of the initial efficiency after 5000 cycles, respectively, while the n-i-p and p-i-n PSCs based on OAI-0 only retain 13% and 37%, respectively, after 2500 cycles."

### "X was calculated to be A, a value that is substantially lower than B measured in Y"
> "The residual stress in the KPP film was calculated to be 7 MPa, a value that is substantially lower than the 67 MPa measured in the Control film."

## D. Gap Statements

Identify what is missing in current knowledge or technology.

### "Despite progress, X remains underexplored"
> "Despite progress, the impact of light cycling remains underexplored, and when simulating outdoor conditions, the high-energy UV light could cause irreversible damage, thereby substantially reducing device performance."

### "Furthermore, X would generate Y. Here [standard tests] are inadequate"
> "Furthermore, cyclic illumination would generate more adverse impacts and accumulate detrimental effects, ultimately shortening the lifespan and reliability of PSCs. Here the standard accelerated ageing tests, such as continuous light soaking, in replicating the outdoor cycling processes, are inadequate."

### "However, there remains room to X before Y"
> "However, there remains room to further increase their efficiency and stability before achieving requirements for commercialization."

### "X has been proven in [field A] but has received limited attention in [field B]"
> "Thermal regulation has been proven important in electronics and silicon solar cells for improving device performance and durability but has received limited attention in mixed Sn-Pb PSCs and tandems."

### "However, less attention has been given to X, which are vital for Y"
> "However, less attention has been given to the perovskite thin film layers, which are sensitive and vital for improving the mechanical reliability and PV performance."

### "X remains unexplored and represents a significant knowledge gap"
> "Notably, the behavior of such materials under thermal cycling tests, particularly their protective effects on PSCs, remains unexplored and represents a significant knowledge gap in this field."

### "Most studies focus on X, while Y remain limited"
> "However, most reported studies focus primarily on initial device performance or environmental stability under moisture or light exposure, while systematic investigations of interfacial robustness under repeated extreme temperature cycling remain limited."

### "Nevertheless, these strategies mostly focus on X, and it is difficult to fundamentally Y"
> "Nevertheless, these strategies mostly focus on enhancing the toughness or slip ability of the film, and it is difficult to fundamentally eliminate the thermal stress caused by CTE mismatch from a physical perspective."

## E. Contribution Statements

Declare what the paper uniquely offers.

### "Here we report [a strategy] to X, achieving simultaneous improvement in A and B"
> "Here we report a strategy to improve the resilience of the grain boundary, achieving simultaneous improvement in the power conversion efficiency and long-term operational durability under realistic light cycling and ultraviolet exposure of PSCs."

### "Here we employ X to Y and show that Z"
> "Here we employ a light-cycling protocol to mimic alternating day-night operation and show that rapid cycling accelerates PSC ageing by promoting internal-stress accumulation and grain-boundary fragmentation in the perovskite absorber."

### "In this study, we demonstrate control over X by Y"
> "In this study, we demonstrate control over the residual stress of perovskite thin films after thermal annealing by incorporating alkyl ammonium molecules, such as n-octylammonium iodide (OAI), into the perovskite precursor."

### "To mitigate X, we introduce Y to Z, while W reinforces V"
> "To mitigate these failure modes, we introduce a co-additive molecular strategy based on lipoic acid, dihydrolipoic acid, and a sulfonium-based derivative to enhance interfacial adhesion, while in situ polymerization during annealing reinforces grain-boundary cohesion."

### "This study presents a [facile] X strategy to Y for fabricating Z"
> "This study presents a facile one-step strategy to integrate potassium pyrophosphate (KPP), an NTE material, into the perovskite precursor for fabricating high-performance FPSCs."

### Concluding impact sentence
> "Our strategy improves the operational stability and commercial viability of triple-cation perovskite photovoltaics."

## F. Transition Phrases

### Pivoting from gap to contribution
- "Here we employ..."
- "In this study, we demonstrate..."
- "To mitigate these failure modes, we introduce..."

### From observation to hypothesis
- "Provided by these analyses, we propose that..."
- "We considered that a [strategy] might reduce [problem]."

### To deeper mechanistic study
- "To gain insight into how the [treatment] affects..."
- "To better understand this behavior, we comprehensively characterized..."
- "To elucidate the passivation mechanism of [X] on [Y], we visualized..."

### Synthesis and conclusion
- "Taken together, our results suggest that..."
- "Collectively, this reversible transformation allows..."

### Future outlook
- "Looking forward, further improvements in [X] may be achieved through..."

### Intra-paragraph emphasis
- "Notably, ..." (extremely common across all journals)
- "Furthermore, ..."
- "In contrast, ..."
- "Specifically, ..."
- "Consistent with our hypothesis, ..."
- "These changes suggest that..."
- "It is worth noting that..."

## G. Hedging Language Examples

Use cautious language when interpreting mechanisms:

> "It would appear that full-spectrum solar light cycling induces permanent changes in the absorber that could critically impact device durability."

> "We considered that a GB inverse thermal expansion-driven film strain-release coupling strategy might reduce dynamic perovskite damage."

> "presumably as a carboxylate via loss of the proton"

> "This may originate from the reduced electron motion path due to the more frequent collision among lattices at a higher temperature."

> "The enhanced thermal stability might stem from the following reasons..."

> "This transient decrease and subsequent recovery in VOC observed for DMSLA-treated devices may arise from interfacial relaxation during repeated thermal cycling."

> "Further studies would be required to determine the exact mechanism."

## H. Confident Assertion Examples

Use confident language for empirical results:

> "Here we report a strategy to improve the resilience of the grain boundary."

> "We further demonstrate that the performance degradation mainly originates from the perovskite layer."

> "We identify that their poor thermal conductivity results in insufficient thermal transfer."

> "Our experiments reveal that thermal exposure duration is more critical than cycle number, with most degradation occurring during initial cycles."

> "These findings conclusively demonstrate that NTE-mediated stress engineering plays a pivotal role in enhancing both the mechanical stability and operational reliability of FPSCs."
